Whistler

Top ski spot.

When you’re looking to take the journey over to Canada, it’s hard to go past Whistler. Consistently ranked as North America’s top ski and snowboard resort, Whistler Blackcomb hosted the 2010 Winter Olympic Games. With over 8000 acres of skiable area, Whistler features two majestic mountains linked together and with the new peak to peak Gondola, accessing both mountains has never been easier. Situated just 2 hours drive from Vancouver and boasting accomodation for all budgets, Whistler features it's renouned village filled with bars, restuarants and shops. You’ve read the articles in the magazines, seen glimpses in the ski and snowboard movies, heard all about it from your friends. Now it’s your turn to experience the magic of Whistler exclusive to Canada.

Whistler features some luxurious resorts reminiscent of a fantasy village.

Niagara Falls

Worlds most renowned waterfall.

Although this part of Canada doesn't boast the freezing cold climate Whistler does it does feature a sight you cannot miss. A natural division between Canada and the US, the massive Niagara Falls is a natural wonder open for everyone. Located on the Niagara River between the states of Ontario and New York, the Niagara Falls are a series of three distinct waterfalls that weave their way around the river. Made up of the American Falls, the Canadian Horseshoe Falls and the Bridal Veil Falls, marvel at the might and beauty of these natural wonders. Options such as venturing on the river cruise are also a must to take in the full Niagra experience. For those wanting a more adrenaline enducing experience a 670 metre zipline is the way to go. Zoom past the Falls while taking in the amazing view.

The Complete Package

What are we offering?

  • 10 Day trip departing from Vancouver.
  • Explore Western Canada's wilderness stopping at stunning viewpoints along the way, and for hiking and swimming.
  • Spend a few days in Whistler in a 4 star resort.
  • Enjoy the winter sports with ski and snowmobile hire included.
  • Tour through Banff and Jasper National Parks.
  • Travel the Icefields Parkway between Lake Louise and Jasper to see Peyto Lake and the Athabasca Glacier.
  • See majestic waterfalls including Bridal Veil Falls, Athabasca Falls and Niagra Falls.
  • Paddleboard on a lake in the Okanagan Valley.

Day 1 - 2

Leaving the city behind, we'll head east through the Fraser Valley. At our first stop, we'll take a short walk in a west coast rainforest to check out Bridal Veil Falls before stopping for lunch in the quaint town of Hope. After crossing through the Coastal Mountains, we arrive in the semi-arid grasslands of the Okanagan where we'll go for a paddleboard on Skaha Lake in Penticton. Then it's onto Kelowna on Lake Okanagan, our destination for the night.

Lake Okanagan

Day 3 - 4

We'll be in two provinces, completely cross three mountain ranges, and travel through four National Parks today as we continue east to Banff. Leaving the Okanagan, we'll stop en route in Revelstoke, where you'll have the option to go lake kayaking in beautiful, hand crafted wooden boats. Continuing onwards, we'll finally enter the Rocky Mountains and cross over the Kicking Horse Pass into Alberta and Banff National Park. Tonight, we'll settle in the historic town of Banff, in the heart of the mountains.

Alberta National Park

Day 5 - 6

Our day begins with visits to the stunning Moraine Lake (seasonal), and one of the world's most famous lakes, Lake Louise Then it's onto the Icefields Parkway, to take in the jagged mountain peaks, glaciers, raging rivers, and more turquoise lakes, all the while keeping your eyes peeled for wildlife. We'll make stops to see the Crowfoot Glacier, Bow Lake, our favourite Peyto Lake, and the impressive Mistaya Canyon, before arriving at our rustic, wilderness hostel alongside Rampart Creek. Why not take a dip in the creek, then relax in the wood-burning sauna, before enjoying a campfire under the stars tonight?

Okanagan Valley

Day 7 - 8

Our journey continues north along the Icefields Parkway, named one of the most scenic drives in the world by National Geographic. Tick another National Park off your list today because we're entering Jasper NP Our first stop in the park is at the Columbia Icefield, the largest icefield in the Rocky Mountains at 325 square kilometres. You'll have the chance to get up close to the Athabasca Glacier, or just enjoy the views from the Discovery Centre. Then it's on to Maligne Canyon, the deepest canyon in Jasper, for a scenic hike that crosses several bridges with amazing views into the canyon. Afterwards, we'll roll into the Jasper townsite where you'll have the rest of the afternoon and evening free to explore on your own.

Columbia Icefield

Day 9 - 10

It's another day of jaw dropping vistas and scenic hikes as we make our way back south. Our first stop is at Athabasca Falls, one of the most powerful and picturesque waterfalls in the Rockies. We'll also stop for a photo opportunity at Tangle Falls, and for an energizing hike at Johnston Canyon, before arriving back in Banff where our tour ends.

Athabasca Falls
