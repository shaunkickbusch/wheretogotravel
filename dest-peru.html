The Amazon

The World's Largest Rainforest.

The Amazon Rainforest is one of the most untouched areas in all of Peru and home to some of the greatest biodiversity on the planet. On a holiday to the Amazon Rainforest you'll trek through damp, dense undergrowth with buzzing insects and hooting animal sounds surrounding you. The Amazon is packed with wildlife, but it can be an infuriating environment in which to spot it. Much is nocturnal and the rest hides in the dense vegetation or in the canopy above. Expert guides miraculously find seemingly invisible creatures, but the attraction is the environment itself in all its vast magnificence. Our best tip is to be prepared. Wear long trousers, comfortable shoes, hats and repellent. Watch where you step and always have your binoculars and camera on you because sometimes - the wildlife comes to find you!

Machu Picchu

The ruins of ancient civilisations.

A spectacularly rich experience in the Andean Mountains awaits you Immerse yourself in the Inca culture as you hike 43 kilometers through the heart of the beautiful Andes. This 4-day trek is the only one to take you along the original Inca Trail; an authentic hand-built stone footpath where you will walk in the footsteps of ancient Inca royalty. Climb from 2,380 meters to 4,210 meters at Dead Woman's Pass, then pass through unforgettably beautiful jungle scenery and hidden labyrinth-style ruins, until you reach the Inti Punku (Sun Gate) in the early morning light. Get ready for awe-inspiring views over Machu Picchu; the most spectacular Inca archaeological site in South America.

Day 1

Your Amazon rainforest holiday in Peru will begin today with your arrival in Lima. We will meet you at the airport and drive you to your hotel in the stylish Miraflores area of the city for you to check in. Depending on your arrival time, you may just want to rest, but if you’re feeling up to it then we’d highly recommend taking a walk around the lovely Parque Kennedy and perhaps to one of our recommended restaurants to sample some of Lima’s world-famous cuisine.

Day 2

This morning we’ll take you back to the airport for a flight over the Andes and down into the Amazon basin. You’ll touch down in the town of Puerto Maldonado and we will drive you the ten minutes to our Puerto Maldonado headquarters for a briefing session before we take you on to the town’s river port for the boat ride along the Madre de Dios river, passing through the indigenous community of the Infierno and the Tambopata National Reserve´s checkpoint and into the buffer zone of this 1.3 million hectare conservation area before we reach the lovely Refugio Amazonas lodge. After dinner we will head out to the river’s edge in the evening, scanning the shores with headlamps and flashlights to catch the red gleams of reflection from caiman eyes.

The Refugio Amazonas lodge is a jungle resort in the Amazon.

Day 3

After breakfast this morning, we will hike through the jungle to your Amazon lodge’s private canopy tower. This is 25m high and built on top of one of the few hills in the area, so that you have an amazing birds-eye view of the primary rainforest. From here get the opportunity to see mixed species canopy flocks as well as toucans, macaws and raptors - don't forget your camera to save some memories of these beautiful birds! Following this we will get ready for a journey further downriver into the deep jungle and the Tambopata Research Centre. After a boat ride, we will cross the confluence with the River Malinowski, and leave the last vestiges of human habitation behind us. From here on in, sightings of capybara, caiman, geese, macaws and other large species will become more frequent. When we arrive we will have lunch and then the lodge manager will welcome us with a short briefing session. We will then leave the lodge and head out on a 3-5k hike which leads us to a look-out point over the River Tambopata. The forest on this trail is where you will get your glimpses at Howler Monkeys as well as Dusky Titi Monkeys.

Spot some Howler Monkeys during your hike.

Day 4

Today will be your last day in the Amazon. Don't threat though - since you'll be heading to Machu Pichu soon! We will take you back upriver to the airport in Puerto Maldonado for your flight to the city of Cusco. We will then pick you up from the airport at Cusco and take you to your hotel. Cusco is formed around a beautiful main square and is small enough to explore the main sites in a day. It's also listed as one of the most attractive cities in South America, with much of the centre comprising of colonial-era buildings with Inca foundations. It's also full of interesting museums, churches and pre-Columbian sites. So get out and explore!

Gusco city looks like a fantasy world - it's so beautiful.

Day 5

You leave Cusco early this morning and drive to the start of the Inca Trail at Piscacucho, commonly known as Km 82. The trail runs alongside the Vilcanota River beneath the impressive snow-capped Nevado Veronica, passing through cactus gardens and fields of corn towards the enormous Inca ruins of Llactapata, where you'll camp near the hamlet of Huayllabamba. Today is mostly flat except for the last few hours, the perfect introduction to the Inca Trail and good practice for the following day.

Huayllabamba has amazing scenary.

Day 6

This is the longest and most strenuous day of your hike where you follow the Inca steps uphill most of the day. A long climb takes you through an area of cloud forest to the meadows of Llulluchapampa, then over the Warmihuañusca (Dead Woman's) Pass, the highest point on the trail at 4,215 metres. After a steep descent, camp in the scenic valley of the Pacamayo river (3,600 metres). It's peaceful and beautiful, with time to reflect on the ingenuity and spirit of the Incas.

Day 7

Today's the day that you've dreamed about and as you descend the world-famous Inca stone stairway past the ruins of Winay Wayna and Inti Punku (The Gate of the Sun) you'll get your first glimpse of the fabled ruined citadel of Machu Picchu. Arrive at The Sun Gate to enjoy unobstructed views of the magnificent ruins, before returning for a dawn tour tomorrow. This is indeed a sight to savour.

Day 8

Get up early to explore Machu Picchu for yourself or take a tour of the ruins with one of the experienced guides. Machu Picchu is spectacular location and is the best known archaeological site on the continent. Be sure to take plenty of photos to show you friends and family back home. After exploring at your leisure, you take the bus back to Aguas Calientes and catch the afternoon train to Ollantaytambo. From here you take a private bus all the way back to Cusco.
