Andaman Embrace Patong Resort

Sprawling resort luxury and ultimate comfort? Andaman Embrace Patong has it all! Set just 1-minute back from Phuket's stunning white Patong Beach and boasting the most spacious Sino-Portuguese guestrooms around, this incredible resort is equal parts peaceful and energising. All travellers will enjoy their stay here. From those who love to lounge all day by the pool or on the beach, to those wanting to stay close to the action, shop and enjoy the region's entertainment areas. Bangla Night Street and the Jungceylon Shopping Centre are just a 10-minute stroll away and the resort is set just 45-mintues from Phuket International Airport. From the moment you touch down, you'll be living the good life there in next to no time!

Phang Nga Bay

Phang Nga Bay is home to the famous landmark called Koh Ta-pu ('Nail Island'). This first found its way onto the international map through its starring role in the James Bond movie 'The Man With the Golden Gun'. And this is where most tours seem to take all of the tourists - all at the same time. The entire area surrounding this island with its signature rocky pinnacle is indeed spectacular, but most of the tours bus visitors up the main highway for an hour on the same well-worn itinerary. An integral part of that tour is lunch at Koh Panyee, a nearby Muslim fishing village.

Bangkok's Grand Palace

If there is one must-see sight that no visit to Bangkok would be complete without, it's the dazzling, spectacular Grand Palace, undoubtedly the city's most famous landmark. Built in 1782 - and for 150 years the home of the Thai King, the Royal court and the administrative seat of government - the Grand Palace of Bangkok is a grand old dame indeed, that continues to have visitors in awe with its beautiful architecture and intricate detail. Within its walls were also the Thai war ministry, state departments, and even the mint. Today, the complex remains the spiritual heart of the Thai Kingdom.
